G-Dragon

Kwon Ji Yong

  • Kwon Ji Yong (born August 18, 1988), better known by his stage name G-Dragon, is a South Korean rapper, singer

    -songwriter and record producer. After 6 years as a trainee under the record label YG Entertainment, G-Dragon officially

    debuted as a member of the South Korean group Big Bang. G-Dragon has been involved with writing, composing, and

    producing many of Big Bang's hit tracks, including "Lies", "Last Farewell", and "Haru Haru."

Taeyang

Dong Young-bae

  • Dong Young-bae (born 18 May 1988), better known by his stage name Taeyang (meaning Sun) or Sol is a South

    Korean singer-songwriter. After appearing in Jinusean's music video "A-yo", Taeyang began training under YG

    Entertainment at the age of 12. After six years of training, he made his debut in 2006 as a member of the South

    Korean boy band Big Bang. His second studio album Rise peaked at #112 on the US Billboard 200 Chart, becoming

    the highest ranking album by a Korean soloist to enter the chart.
